Data Accuracy and Limitations

Satellite Detection Limitations

Our fire detection data comes primarily from NASA's FIRMS (Fire Information for Resource Management System), which uses satellite imagery to detect thermal anomalies. This system has significant limitations:

Time Delays

Fire detection data can be delayed by 3-12 hours depending on satellite pass times and data processing. Fast-moving fires may not appear until hours after ignition.

Weather Interference

Clouds, heavy smoke, and atmospheric conditions can prevent satellites from detecting fires, resulting in gaps in coverage during critical periods.

Size Thresholds

Satellites can only detect fires above certain size thresholds. Small fires, spot fires, and fires in their early stages may not be detected.

False Positives

The system may detect other heat sources as fires, including industrial facilities, gas flares, volcanoes, and reflected sunlight.

Geographic Limitations

Satellite coverage varies by location. Some remote areas may have limited or delayed coverage, especially at high latitudes.

Extinguished Fires

The system may continue to show fires that have been extinguished, as satellite data reflects conditions from hours earlier.
