The LA and Pacific Palisades Fires of January 2025

The Palisades and Eaton Fires, which ignited on January 7, 2025, caused unprecedented devastation across the Los Angeles area, including Pacific Palisades, Malibu, Altadena, and Pasadena. Fueled by dry conditions and strong winds, these fires destroyed over 12,000 structures, claimed at least 24 lives, and displaced thousands, according to Los Angeles County officials. The Palisades Fire alone scorched beachfront properties along the Pacific Coast Highway, while the Eaton Fire ravaged hillside communities in Altadena. At Eye on the Fire LLC, we are committed to raising awareness about the importance of fire safety and preparedness. Below are personal photos and videos capturing the impact of these fires, shared to highlight the need for vigilance and community support. Fire Safety Tips for Urban Wildfires

The LA and Pacific Palisades fires highlight the unique challenges of urban wildfires, where densely populated areas meet flammable vegetation. Here are some fire safety tips to help you prepare:

  • Create a Defensible Space: Clear flammable materials like dry leaves and brush within 30 feet of your home, especially in areas like Pacific Palisades where homes border wildland. Learn more from Cal Fireâ€™s Defensible Space Guide.
  • Prepare an Evacuation Plan: Have multiple escape routes planned, as roads like the Pacific Coast Highway can become congested during evacuations. Visit Ready.gov for evacuation planning tips.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor real-time fire updates to stay ahead of rapidly spreading fires like the Eaton Fire in Altadena.
  • Assemble an Emergency Kit: Include essentials like water, food, masks (for smoke inhalation), and important documents. The American Red Cross provides a detailed checklist.
