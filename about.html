Your trusted resource for real-time US wildfire tracking and fire safety. Learn how we empower communities with interactive maps and preparedness guides.

Our Mission

Eye on the Fire is dedicated to providing real-time wildfire monitoring and comprehensive fire safety resources to communities across the United States.

At Eye on the Fire, our mission is to provide accurate, real-time wildfire tracking across the United States, empowering individuals and communities to stay informed and safe. By consolidating data from trusted sources like NASA FIRMS and NIFC, we deliver an interactive US wildfire map that helps you monitor fires near you and access critical safety resources.

We are committed to enhancing wildfire preparedness through comprehensive guides on home hardening, defensible space, and emergency planning, ensuring you have the tools to protect what matters most.

With more than 70,000 wildfires burning approximately 7 million acres in the United States annually, our mission goes beyond tracking - it's about building resilient communities through education, preparedness, and real-time information.

Why Choose Eye on the Fire?

Our platform combines cutting-edge technology with expert knowledge to create the most comprehensive wildfire monitoring and safety resource available.

  • Real-Time Wildfire Monitoring: Our interactive map provides up-to-date wildfire data, helping you track fires near you anywhere in the US. We update every 15 minutes from NASA FIRMS satellite data.
  • User-Friendly Interface: Easily navigate our map with controls to locate your position, zoom into regions, and explore fire details with our intuitive design built for all ages and technical abilities.
  • Comprehensive Safety Resources: Access expert guides on wildfire prevention, home safety, and emergency preparedness to protect your family and property, developed by fire safety professionals.
  • Trusted Data Sources: We partner with NASA FIRMS, NIFC, NOAA, and other official channels to ensure reliable information and cross-reference multiple data sources for accuracy.
  • Mobile Accessibility: Access our platform on any device, with our responsive design ensuring you can stay informed whether you're at home or on the move.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Eye on the Fire and our wildfire tracking services.

Our wildfire map is updated every 15 minutes with the latest satellite data from NASA FIRMS (Fire Information for Resource Management System). Active fire information is collected by MODIS (Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) and VIIRS (Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite) thermal sensors aboard NASA satellites.

Additionally, we incorporate data from NIFC (National Interagency Fire Center) and local fire agencies for comprehensive coverage of wildfires across the United States.

While we don't currently offer a native mobile app, our website is fully optimized for mobile devices. You can access all of our features, including the interactive wildfire map, preparation guides, and emergency resources, from any smartphone or tablet.

Simply bookmark our website for quick access. We're exploring the development of native mobile applications for iOS and Android in the future.

Eye on the Fire uses multiple trusted data sources to ensure high accuracy. Our primary data comes from NASA FIRMS satellite detection, which has a global accuracy of approximately 80% for active fire detection. We enhance this with ground-truth data from NIFC and local fire agencies.

While all remote sensing systems have limitations due to factors like cloud cover and satellite orbital patterns, we employ verification algorithms and multiple data sources to provide the most reliable information possible.

It's important to note that our service is designed to be informational and should be used alongside official evacuation notices and emergency alerts from your local authorities.

Currently, Eye on the Fire focuses exclusively on wildfire tracking and preparedness resources for the United States, including all 50 states and territories. This allows us to provide highly specialized, localized information and integrate with US-specific emergency management systems.

We're continually evaluating opportunities to expand our coverage to other countries in the future.

There are several ways to contribute to our mission:

  • Share our resources with your community, especially in fire-prone regions
  • Participate in our community forums and share your wildfire preparedness experiences
  • Report any data discrepancies or satellite detection errors you notice
  • If you're a fire professional or emergency manager, contact us about partnership opportunities
  • Support our work through donations that help us maintain and improve our tracking systems
