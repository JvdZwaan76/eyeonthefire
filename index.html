Essential Fire Prevention Tips

Proactive fire safety starts at home. Protect your property and community by implementing these essential fire prevention practices. Reducing fuel sources is key to improving your home's chances of surviving a wildfire.

Clear Defensible Space

Maintain a minimum of 30 feet (ideally 100 feet) of lean, clean, and green space around your home. Remove dead vegetation, dry leaves, and overhanging branches to reduce potential fuel for wildfires.

Maintain Roof & Gutters

Regularly clean roofs and gutters of pine needles, leaves, and other flammable debris. Embers can easily ignite these materials. Use non-combustible roofing materials where possible.

Fire-Resistant Landscaping

Choose native, drought-tolerant, and fire-resistant plants for landscaping within your defensible space. Avoid highly flammable plants like junipers or bamboo near structures.
